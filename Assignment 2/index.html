What are tacos?

Tacos are one of the most popular street foods in Mexico, and although the origin isn’t known for sure, the first record of tacos was called the “taco de minero,” or miner’s taco, referring to 18th century silver mines. Most traditional types of tacos are made with small, soft corn tortillas that are warmed and folded around a variety of fillings (no hard-shell tacos here!). Some recipes even use two tortillas to prevent the tacos from tearing. You’ll find an array of flavorful fillings like beef, pork, fish, or vegetables. No matter which type of taco you prefer, be sure to top them with these traditional, zesty garnishes: onion, cilantro, and lime juice.

About our restaurant

Tica's Tacos, fast-food restaurant chain headquartered in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, that offers Mexican-inspired foods. Founded in 2010 by Australian entrepreneur Angel Tica, the chain now has more than 8 locations nationwide.

History & Development of Tica's Taco

Way back in 2010, Angel Tica (an accountant) was struggling to satisfy her pretty serious craving for some deliciously fresh Mexican food. So she set off on a scoping trip to Mexico, sampling the best of the best culinary delights the country has to offer (in a street food kind of way).

Full of ideas, dreams, tacos and tequila, Angel returned to Australia, and set about bringing her vision of creating the best Mexican restaurant in Australia to life. Within 6 months, she had herself a sweet little restaurant on Swan Street with an hour-long queue and a couple of maxed out credit cards.

The good word spread fast, and as demand grew, so did Tica's network. Nowadays, we serve up our legendary tacos and margs to thousands of guests weekly, with 8 restaurants scattered across Melbourne and Sydney.

Available types of tacos

Carne Asada

Carne asada—meaning “grilled meat”—is a taco made from thin slices of steak that have been marinated and grilled. Use flank steak or skirt steak and slice it into bite-sized chunks.

Zauberman, Kara. “Eat Your Way through This Guide of Different Types of Tacos.” The Pioneer Woman, 5 Apr. 2021, www.thepioneerwoman.com/food-cooking/meals-menus/g36008054/types-of-tacos/. Accessed 27 Nov. 2021.

Shrimp Taco

A traditional taco from Mexico’s Pacific coast, camarones (or shrimp tacos) are made with flavorful shrimp and fresh toppings. It’s perfect for a healthy, quick-cooking, weeknight dinner.

Zauberman, Kara. “Eat Your Way through This Guide of Different Types of Tacos.” The Pioneer Woman, 5 Apr. 2021, www.thepioneerwoman.com/food-cooking/meals-menus/g36008054/types-of-tacos/. Accessed 27 Nov. 2021.

Barbacoa

You’ll need an underground oven to make this traditional taco. The term “barbacoa” refers to the way the meat (usually lamb or goat) is prepared—topped with leaves and slowly cooked in a pit dug into the earth. To make barbacoa at home, you can use a slow cooker for tender, juicy tacos.
